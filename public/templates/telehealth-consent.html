What Is Telehealth?

Telehealth is the delivery of therapy services using interactive audio, video, or other electronic communications between a provider and a patient who are not in the same physical location. This may include video sessions, telephone sessions, and secure messaging.

Benefits of Telehealth

  • Greater convenience and access to care from your home or another private location.
  • Reduced travel time and related costs.
  • Continuity of care during illness, travel, or weather disruptions.

Risks and Limitations

  • Technology problems (poor connection, dropped calls) may interrupt sessions.
  • Telehealth is not appropriate for all clinical situations, especially crises or severe symptoms.
  • Despite reasonable security precautions, electronic communications may be intercepted by unauthorized persons.
  • Non-verbal cues may be harder to read than in person.

Confidentiality and Security

Your therapist uses HIPAA-compliant platforms for video sessions. You are responsible for finding a private, quiet space for your sessions. Do not record sessions without your therapist's written permission.

Emergency Procedures

Telehealth is not suitable for emergencies. If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, call 988 (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline), 911, or go to your nearest emergency room. At the start of each session, you may be asked to confirm your physical location so emergency services can be directed if needed.

Location Requirements

You must be physically located in a state where your therapist is licensed to practice at the time of each telehealth session. If you plan to travel, please notify your therapist in advance so we can confirm whether services can continue.

Technical Requirements

You will need a reliable internet connection, a device with a camera and microphone (for video sessions), and a quiet, private space. Please log in a few minutes early to test your connection.

Consent

By signing the acknowledgment in the patient portal, you confirm that you have read and understand this telehealth consent, have had the opportunity to ask questions, and agree to receive telehealth services under the terms described above.

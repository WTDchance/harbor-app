Informed Consent for Therapy Services

A standard informed consent template — please customize for your practice before sharing with patients.

For your therapist: This is a generic starter template. Update the language to reflect your therapeutic approach, fees, and policies before going live with real patients.

Welcome

This document provides important information about the therapeutic services you are about to receive. Please read it carefully and ask any questions you may have before signing.

Nature of Therapy

Therapy is a collaborative process between you and your therapist. The goal is to help you better understand yourself, develop coping skills, and work toward positive change. Therapy can address emotional, behavioral, and relationship concerns. Sessions typically last 45–55 minutes and frequency is determined by clinical need.

Risks and Benefits

Therapy can lead to meaningful improvements in mood, relationships, and overall well-being. However, it may also bring up uncomfortable feelings such as sadness, anxiety, anger, or frustration as you discuss difficult experiences. There is no guarantee that therapy will produce specific results. Your therapist will work with you to address any concerns that arise during treatment.

Confidentiality

Information shared in therapy is confidential and protected by law. However, there are exceptions where your therapist is legally and ethically required to disclose information without your consent:

  • If you present an imminent danger to yourself or others.
  • If there is reasonable suspicion of abuse or neglect of a child, elder, or dependent adult.
  • If a court of law subpoenas your records.
  • If required for billing or insurance reimbursement (limited information).

Fees and Payment

Session fees, sliding-scale options, and accepted insurance plans will be discussed with you directly. Payment is generally expected at the time of service. Please ask if you have questions about cost or coverage.

Cancellation Policy

If you need to cancel or reschedule an appointment, please provide as much notice as possible. Late cancellations or no-shows may be subject to a fee. See your practice's cancellation policy for details.

Contact Between Sessions

Your therapist may be reached by phone, text, or secure messaging for scheduling and brief check-ins. Please note that email and text are not fully secure communication methods. In case of emergency, call 988, 911, or go to your nearest emergency room.

Ending Therapy

You have the right to end therapy at any time. If you choose to discontinue, your therapist may request a final session to discuss progress and provide referrals if appropriate.

Consent

By signing the acknowledgment in the patient portal, you confirm that you have read and understand this consent form, have had the opportunity to ask questions, and agree to the terms of treatment described above.
